What Makes NPC Interactions Engaging?

Motivation

xGaming is a startup trying to develop tools to make game development easier. One problem in game development is generating new content -- for example, creating new quests for the player, as distributed by NPCs.

How can we automatically generate immersive NPCs that give players quests while feeling like a real character?

A rough progression of NPC depth

These elements help give an NPC depth.

  1. Dialogue and quests are pre-programmed, such as in Skyrim
  2. NPC dialogue references the state of the world as it is
  3. NPCs have limited perception of the world; e.g. they 'learn' information from what the player or even other NPCs 'tell' them
  4. NPCs remember past events and reference them in dialogue
  5. NPCs have goals (i.e. a desired world state) and are happy/sad about it
  6. NPCs ask players (or other NPCs?) to help them achieve their goal (i.e. quests)
  7. NPCs can interact with the world in any way the player can, in order to achieve their goals
  8. NPCs have simulated internal state (emotions), which gives them moods
  9. NPCs' internal stimuli are not perfectly attuned to their environment, making them irrational
  10. NPCs carry emotional baggage, making them sensitive to certain stimuli
