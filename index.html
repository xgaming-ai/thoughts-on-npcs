What Makes NPC Interactions Engaging?

Motivation

xGaming is a startup trying to develop tools to make game development easier. One problem in game development is generating new content -- for example, creating new quests for the player, as distributed by NPCs. So, let's ask ourselves...

How can we automatically generate immersive, quest-giving NPCs that feel like a real character in a real world?

A rough progression of NPC depth

In classical RPGs, such as Skyrim, NPC quests and dialogue are pre-programmed. I propose that, instead, NPCs become more akin to real agents in the world, with goals, perception, memory, and possibly even emotions such that any conversation with an NPC is entirely dependent on the state of the world. This will do more to make them immersive than any amount of surface-level linguistic styling.

These elements help give an NPC depth.

  1. NPC dialogue references the state of the world as it is
  2. NPCs have limited perception of the world; e.g. they 'learn' information from what the player or even other NPCs 'tell' them
  3. NPCs remember past events and reference them in dialogue
  4. NPCs have goals (i.e. a desired world state) and are happy/sad about it
  5. NPCs ask players (or other NPCs?) to help them achieve their goal (i.e. quests)
  6. NPCs treat players differently depending on how players' competence and attitude toward the NPC
  7. NPCs can move through the world, which influences their perception of the world
  8. NPCs can interact with the world in any way the player can, in order to achieve their goals
  9. NPCs have simulated internal state (emotions), which gives them moods and multiple (and possibly conflicting) goals
  10. Different NPCs exhibit different emotional behaviors based on arbitrarily weighted 'personalities'
  11. NPCs' internal stimuli are not perfectly attuned to their environment, making them irrational
  12. NPCs carry emotional baggage, making them sensitive to certain stimuli

NPC depth, simplified

In other words, to maximize NPC immersion in dialogue, they should:

  1. Perceive and remember the state of the world (imperfectly)
  2. Have goals and value judgements, which the player can help or hinder
  3. Have an internal emotional state and personality
  4. Be capable of interacting with the world (and other NPCs) any way the player can

How do we make a demo of this?

I have been tasked with creating a ROBLOX game to demonstrate the value of an AI dialogue system. There are significant constraints:

  • I don't quite know the specific boundaries of our current AI dialogue technology.
  • I have eight weeks to make this demo game.
  • Some NPC behaviors described above are beyond the limits of current technology and may be significantly difficult to create.

At the same time, xGaming will fail as a startup if it does not solve a sufficiently valuable (and presumably difficult) problem. Ideally there is a sweet spot that optimizes NPC immersion with the project constraints.

It's also possible that a relatively low-immersion NPC demo may be successful! Regardless, I am inclined to make the NPCs as immersive as possible within my ability.
